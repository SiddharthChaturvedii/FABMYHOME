Strategic UI Placement for EngagementTo ensure the "pop" leads to actual user interactions, follow these placement strategies:1. The "Hero" Interaction (Orange)In a luxury context, orange should be used sparingly like a gold leaf.CTA Buttons: Use Burnt Terracotta for "Book a Consultation" or "Add to Moodboard." Because orange naturally draws the eye, it should be reserved for the highest-value actions.Active States: When a user selects a piece of furniture, a thin orange border or a small "selected" badge creates an immediate sense of warmth and achievement.2. The AI & Navigation Layer (Teal)Teal represents trust and technology, making it perfect for your AI features.AI Visualizer Toggle: Use Electric Cyan-Teal for the "AR View" or "AI Room Design" buttons. This differentiates "functional" tech features from "shopping" actions.Micro-interactions: Use teal for hover states on navigation links and progress bars. It feels "cool" and responsive, encouraging the user to keep clicking through the site.3. Background Depth (Deep Teal)Footer & Header: A Deep Midnight Teal background with white or orange text feels much more "premium" than standard black or white. It frames the high-quality product images of the furniture.Section Breaks: Use deep teal for the "Personalized for You" section. Dark backgrounds create a "VIP lounge" feel that increases the perceived value of the items shown.UI Engagement TipsThe 60-30-10 Rule: Aim for 60% Neutral (Alabaster/White), 30% Deep Teal (Foundational), and 10% Orange (The Pop). This prevents the UI from looking like a sports brand.Gradients: Use a subtle linear gradient from Deep Teal to Electric Teal on your AI-powered search bars or chat icons. This suggests "intelligence" and modern capability.Neumorphism: Since you are interested in clean designs, apply subtle shadows on your product cards. A soft shadow with a hint of teal depth makes the furniture images look like they are floating in a real space.
